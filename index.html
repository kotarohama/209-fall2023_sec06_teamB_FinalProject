About Our Website

Project Concept

We analyzed the generation of values associated with signing free agent players from various analytical perspectives. Users can interact with each visualization and obtain a custom-filtered view to gain better insights and, ultimately, make more informed decisions.

Intended Users

The dashboard is designed to cater to a diverse audience, spanning from casual fans to seasoned experts. It aspires to become a valuable tool for not only fantasy baseball team players but also hold potential utility for MLB general managers.

How to Navigate

Each panel within the "Analysis" section will open a window with an embedded visualization. Users can explore three different types of analysis. The "Data" section provides details on the source of the data used to generate each visualization.

Our Data

Over 7,000 data points from a trusted source.

Datasource

Our analysis utilizes individual player data from Baseball-Reference.com, specifically focusing on Free Agent players.

Period

Our analysis covers data from 2004 to 2023, focusing on the more trustworthy and relevant Free Agency data for today's context.

Metrics

Our analysis considers two crucial metrics: Salary, the annual earnings of Free Agent players, and Win-Above-Replacement (WAR), which measures a player's performance compared to a replacement-level player in the same position.

Are major free agents worth the money?

In this analysis, Major Free Agents are defined as individual players whose contracts (salaries) surpass the 95th percentile of salaries across the entire league.

Metric Definitions:
"Overpaid" - Top 5% Salary & Below Top 5% WaR
"Regular" - Below Top 5% Salary & Below Top 5% WaR
"TopStars" - Top 5% Salary & Top 5% WaR
"Undervalued" - Below 5% Salary & Top 5% WaR
